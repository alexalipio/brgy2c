Programs & Services

Education

Gentri believes that providing care and support is an equal and important component of education. This is the heart of the “Educare” program founded with assistance from the Luis IV Ferrer Foundation designed to provide a better head start for underprivileged children of the city. Nursery students are given free milk and nutritional food feeding. Free uniforms and school supplies are given to elementary and high school children.

Clean and Green

From being a backwater municipality to becoming one of the thriving cities in the metro, Gentri has maintained its stand on protecting the environment even before the “go green” trend.

Health

Gentri prides itself on prioritizing health care, especially during the term of then Mayor Luis IV Ferrer. With the belief that the foundation of good governance is through healthy citizenry, the local government created next-door health services of affordable quality.

Health Care: Green Card

Worried about expensive hospital bills? Looking for an affordable health care plan? Residents of Gentri need not look far, as they can avail of a free hospitalization package worth Php 30,000, care of the local city government.

Tourism and Cultural

In an effort to prime Gentri as a tourism and cultural site to attract local and foreign investors, several beautification projects are being implemented throughout the city. The flagship project, the Gentri Historical Corridor Project is an ambitious restoration and preservation project involves physical restoration and transformation highlighting our rich cultural heritage.

Manpower Training Development and Employment

To ensure that Las Pinas residents are provided with employable skills, manpower training programs have been undertaken by the Aguilar administration since 1995. Manpower training and livelihood centers and its satellite offices in the barangays benefit more than 2,000 members every year. There are a variety of training courses to choose from: electronics, dressmaking and tailoring, stuffed-toy making, driving, radio-TV repair, computer education and other entrepreneurial skills.

The City of General Trias

Originally named after its Patron Saint, Francis of Assisi, 1181-1226, the municipality of General Trias was founded in December 13, 1748. The original name of the town was changed to General Trias in honor of Gen. Mariano Trias (1869-1914), by virtue of a law fathered by Emilio P. Virata during his term as representative of Cavite, 1919-1921.
